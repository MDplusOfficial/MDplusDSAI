About the Datathon

What is the MD+ Datathon?

The 4th annual MD+ Datathon is a national, month-long event that brings medical students from various backgrounds together to explore complex healthcare challenges and create data-driven solutions. Hosted by MD+ and its partners, the datathon encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, innovative thinking, and real-world problem solving using clinical datasets. This year, the theme is Empowering Patients Through AI.

Why "Empowering Patients Through AI"?

Students are encouraged to interpret the theme broadly and creatively, drawing on their unique experiences and interests to define what "empowering patients" means in a data-driven context.

Some examples include health literacy, access to care, after-visit summaries, chatbots from chronic health conditions, insurance navigation, language accessibility, etc.

Who should join?

Medical students, residents, and graduate students pursuing degrees related to human health. Teams will consist of 3-6 trainees. Multidisciplinary teams are highly encouraged.

What you'll get

Skills in data analysis and machine learning, experience tackling high-impact problems in patient care, and opportunities to publish work.

Additionally, the winners will receive a monetary prize!

FAQs

I have little/no data science or computational experience. Can I still participate?

Absolutely! Learning the computational tools is half the fun of the event. Participants will have access to tutorials walking through the basics of Python and R, and also how to go about analyzing the dataset. Datathon submissions are also judged on more than just technical complexity. In fact, datathon judges care more about the insights derived and the data analysis than the computational novelty or complexity of the project!

What is the time commitment?

We encourage all participants to attend the kickoff event and the pitch competition, but other than that, there are no required synchronous events. The time commitment is as much as you and your team want to put into the project!

What is the final product we are submitting?

Your team will submit a 1-2 page report of your findings along with the code you used.

Do I need a team beforehand?

No. Search for teammates by adding your name and bio to these slides.

Can I see previous examples of projects?

Yes! Last year, our theme was "Responsible Generative AI," and all teams had the option to publish an abstract-version of their findings in PRISM's special edition journal. Read the work here.

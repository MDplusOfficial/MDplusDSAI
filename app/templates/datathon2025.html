About the Datathon

What is MD+ Datathon?

The 4th annual MD+ Datathon is a national, month-long event that brings medical students from various backgrounds together to explore complex healthcare challenges and create data-driven solutions. Hosted by MD+ and its partners, the datathon encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, innovative thinking, and real-world problem solving using clinical datasets.

Why "Empowering Patients Through AI"?

Students are encouraged to interpret the theme broadly and creatively, drawing on their unique experiences and interests to define what "empowering patients" means in a data-driven context.

Some examples include health literacy, access to care, after-visit summaries, chatbots from chronic health conditions, insurance navigation, language accessibility, etc.

Who should join?

Medical students, residents, and graduate students pursuing degrees related to human health. Multidisciplinary teams are highly encouraged.

What you'll get

Skills in data analysis and machine learning, experience tackling high-impact problems in patient care, and opportunities to publish work.

Additionally, the winners will receive a monetary prize!

FAQs

Do I need a team beforehand?

No. Search for teammates by adding your name and bio to these slides.

Will data be de‑identified?

Yes. All shared datasets are de‑identified and governed by event data use policies.

What about IP?

Teams retain IP. Sponsors may offer optional collaboration agreements for pilots.

Is this beginner‑friendly?

Absolutely. We provide starter notebooks, mentor office hours, and UX templates.
